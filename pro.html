The Predator-Prey
Ecosystem

Discover how nature balances predator and prey populations through the Lotka-Volterra equations one of the most elegant models in mathematical biology.

🌍 The Story Introduction

Imagine a forest with rabbits and wolves. When there are many rabbits, wolves have plenty of food, so the wolf population grows. But as more wolves hunt, the rabbit population drops. With fewer rabbits to eat, wolves begin to starve and their numbers fall. With fewer wolves, rabbits can safely reproduce again — and the cycle repeats.

💡 Key Insight

The predator population always lags behind the prey. When prey is abundant, predators grow — but by the time they peak, prey has already started declining.

🎛️ The Parameters Deep Dive

α Prey Growth Rate

How fast prey reproduce without predators.

β Predation Rate

How effectively predators hunt prey.

γ Predator Death Rate

How quickly predators die without food.

δ Conversion Efficiency

How well predators convert prey into offspring.

🔄 Oscillation Phases Dynamics

  • Phase 1: Abundant prey → predators growing → prey starts declining
  • Phase 2: Predators peak → prey crashing → predators start starving
  • Phase 3: Both populations low → predators die off
  • Phase 4: Predators scarce → prey recovers → cycle restarts

✦ Particle Visualization Advanced View

Beyond the standard trajectory, the ✦ Particles mode offers a stochastic look at the ecosystem. This replaces the mathematical point with a dynamic swarm of probabilistic particles.

💡 Density Mapping

The number of particles in the swarm is tied directly to the instantaneous population count. This provides an intuitive visual for the biological mass of the system at any point in the cycle, making it easier to see the scale of population booms and crashes.
